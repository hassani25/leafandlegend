The Problem

An Industry Stuck at the Beginning of Its Own Value Chain

For generations, Morocco has been one of the world's largest producers of cannabis resin — yet the country captures only a fraction of the value its plant creates. The raw material is grown here, harvested here, and then exported abroad, where others transform it into finished products sold at many times the original price. Morocco imports back the very products made from its own plant.

The 2021 legalization law created a framework for legal cannabis activity, but the industry remains overwhelmingly focused on cultivation and raw material supply. The next step — the one that creates lasting value, skilled jobs, and exportable intellectual property — is product development. That step has barely begun.

A country that grows the raw material should not be a spectator in the products made from it.

The Approach

Build With Partners.
Validate Before Scaling.

Leaf & Legend does not claim capabilities it has not yet built. We are honest about where we are today and transparent about what comes next. Every step depends on finding the right partners and meeting the right standards.

⚙

External Expertise

Product development requires specialized knowledge in formulation, delivery systems, and regulatory compliance. We intend to work with qualified external partners — formulation experts, contract manufacturers, and analytical laboratories — rather than building every capability in-house.

Partnerships being explored
⚗

Scientific Rigor

We believe consumer products derived from cannabis must be developed with the same rigor expected of any health-adjacent product. That means analytical testing of raw materials, standardized formulation processes, and quality controls. We intend to work with facilities that meet transformation and extraction requirements, and ultimately with GMP-aligned partners for manufacturing.

GMP alignment intended, not yet achieved
★

Transparent Development

We will not market products before they are ready. We will not claim partnerships that do not exist. We will not imply regulatory approvals that have not been obtained. Our development process will be documented and transparent. We are exploring academic collaboration opportunities and are interested in future observation programs — but none are confirmed at this time.

Commitment in practice

The Heritage

Generations of Knowledge.
One Clear Direction.

Walid Hassani grew up in a multi-generational Moroccan cannabis family — not in a laboratory, not in a boardroom, but in the fields and communities where cannabis has been part of life for centuries. That heritage gives an understanding of the plant that cannot be learned from textbooks alone.

But heritage without direction is nostalgia. The direction is clear: transform traditional knowledge into modern, useful products. Move from raw material to finished goods. Create value where the plant grows, not just where it is processed.

The vision is not simply to sell cannabis. The vision is to transform a traditional plant into useful products that improve people's lives.
